For this piece, the lettering was placed horizontally across the lower back. This placement provides enough space for detailed typography while maintaining balance and readability.

## The client's design request

The client wanted a tattoo that represented a meaningful personal influence without becoming overly decorative.

### Using a name as the focal point

Instead of adding illustrations or ornamental elements, the entire composition focuses on the name itself. This approach keeps the tattoo direct, personal, and easy to recognize.

### Choosing the Old English style

Old English lettering remains one of the most popular styles for statement tattoos because of its bold strokes, historical character, and Gothic influence.

For this tattoo, the style helped create a strong visual identity while preserving the readability of the name.

### Adjusting spacing and proportions

Lettering tattoos require careful spacing and line weight adjustments. The composition was customized to ensure that the details remain clear after healing and continue to age well over time.

## Why the lower back works well for lettering tattoos

The lower back offers enough horizontal space for names, quotes, and personal phrases.

For male clients especially, this placement can create a strong and balanced composition while remaining relatively private in daily life.

Positioning the tattoo near the Venus dimples also helps soften the overall composition and follow the natural body lines.

## Things to consider before getting a lettering tattoo

If you plan to tattoo the name of an artist, musician, or someone who has personally inspired you, readability should always come before decoration.

With Old English lettering, choosing the right size is essential because overly small details may become difficult to read after healing.
