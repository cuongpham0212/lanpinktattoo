## What should you prepare before booking?

Before booking, prepare your tattoo idea, preferred placement, estimated size, reference images, and any meaning you want the design to carry.

### Details that help the artist quote accurately

- tattoo idea
- placement
- approximate size
- preferred style
- reference images
- budget range if available

## What clients often overlook

A good tattoo design is not only about the image itself. Placement, body flow, size, and healing also affect how the final tattoo looks.

## Booking vs consultation: what is the difference?

A consultation helps the artist understand your idea first. A tattoo appointment should only be confirmed when the placement, size, style, and budget are clear.

### When should you ask for a consultation first?

- you have several references but feel unsure
- you want a cover up or tattoo restoration
- you are unsure about placement
- you want a custom design
- you need help matching the design with your budget

## A useful booking tip

Send both references you like and references you do not like. This helps the artist understand your taste much faster.

## What happens after you book?

After you send a booking request, Lan Pink reviews your idea, placement, size, and style before confirming the next step. For custom tattoos, the artist may ask a few extra questions first.

### Common booking mistakes

- sending a design that is too detailed for the size
- not knowing the placement
- not sharing approximate size
- forgetting to send the old tattoo photo for cover ups
- booking too close to the desired date

## When should you book early?

Book early if you want a large tattoo, custom design, pet memorial tattoo, or anything that needs extra design preparation.
