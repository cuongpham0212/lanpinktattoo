## Does getting a tattoo hurt?

Yes, getting a tattoo involves some discomfort, but the pain level depends on the placement, tattoo size, session length, and your personal pain tolerance.

Bony areas such as ribs, ankles, fingers, collarbone, and feet usually feel more intense. Fleshy areas such as the outer arm, forearm, calf, and thigh are usually easier for beginners.

For a deeper explanation, read our guide: [Does getting a tattoo hurt?](/en/tattoo-guide/does-getting-a-tattoo-hurt/)

## What hurts more: lining or shading?

Lining and shading feel different.

Lining usually feels sharper, more direct, and more intense at the beginning. Shading often feels warmer, more irritating, and more tiring over time, especially when the skin has already become sensitive.

You can learn more here: [Lining needles vs shading needles: which hurts more?](/en/tattoo-guide/lining-vs-shading-needles-which-hurts-more/)

## How long does a tattoo take to heal?

Most tattoos take around 2–4 weeks for the surface skin to heal. Deeper skin recovery can take longer.

Healing time depends on tattoo size, placement, your skin condition, aftercare routine, and daily activity.

During healing, avoid picking, scratching, swimming, and exposing the tattoo to strong sunlight.

---

## How do I take care of a new tattoo?

Basic tattoo aftercare usually includes:

- Keep the tattoo clean
- Wash gently as instructed
- Pat dry with clean tissue or towel
- Apply a thin layer of recommended aftercare product
- Avoid scratching or picking
- Avoid swimming until healed
- Avoid direct sun exposure

Always follow the aftercare instructions given by your tattoo artist, because aftercare may vary depending on tattoo size and placement.

---

## Can I exercise after getting a tattoo?

Light daily movement is usually fine, but intense workouts should be avoided during the early healing stage, especially if the tattoo is in an area that stretches, sweats, or rubs against clothing.

Avoid gym equipment contact, heavy sweating, swimming, and activities that irritate the tattoo until it has healed properly.

---

## Can I shower after getting a tattoo?

Yes, but keep it gentle.

Avoid soaking the tattoo, hot baths, swimming pools, and long showers during the early healing period. Do not scrub the tattoo directly.

---

## Can I go swimming after getting a tattoo?

No. Swimming should be avoided until the tattoo is fully healed.

Pools, beaches, rivers, and hot tubs can irritate the skin and increase the risk of healing problems.

---

## Can I expose my tattoo to the sun?

Avoid direct sun exposure while the tattoo is healing.

After the tattoo has healed, sunscreen can help protect the tattoo from fading. Sun exposure is one of the main reasons tattoos lose sharpness and color over time.

---

## Do I need a touch-up?

Not every tattoo needs a touch-up. It depends on skin type, placement, healing process, aftercare, and tattoo style.

Fine Line and small detailed tattoos may sometimes need minor adjustments after healing.

If needed, a touch-up is usually done only after the skin has fully healed.

---

## Can I get a tattoo if I have sensitive skin?

Possibly, but you should discuss your skin condition with the artist before booking. If you have allergies, active skin irritation, or medical concerns, it is better to consult a qualified healthcare professional first.

## Can old tattoos be covered up?

Yes, some old tattoos can be covered or redesigned, depending on the size, darkness, placement, and condition of the existing tattoo.

Cover-up work usually requires a consultation before confirming the final design.

## Can tattoos be removed?

Tattoo removal is possible with laser treatment, but it can take multiple sessions and may cost more than the original tattoo.

If you are unsure about a design, it is better to take more time before getting tattooed.
